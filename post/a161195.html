Research

  1. Mechanized Semantics and Verified Compilation for a Data flow Synchronous Language with Reset (POPL ‘20)

    • Type: paper reading
    • Conference: POPL ‘20
    • State: the second pass (the three-pass approach)
    • Notes: This paper is recommended by my mentor for knowing the state-of-the-art research in fields such as stream languages and verified compilation. My later work will be built on Lustre V6 which is also introduced in this paper. Specifications based on block diagrams and state machines are used to design control software, especially in the certified development of safety-critical applications. Dataflow Synchronous Language like Lustre is useful for programmers to translate such specifications into executable code. This paper extend the former specification and verification of a compiler for Lustre in Coq to treat a primitive for resetting subsystems. (Comment: Confusing? What is the extension? Why the former method is not sufficient?)

  2. The Lustre V6 Reference Manual

    • Type: related research
    • Notes: With the help of the manual, I had an overview of the Lustre Language including its design and basic features.Following the description of the syntax rules in the second chapter, I was able to implement the AST(Abstract Syntax Tree) of Lustre V6 in OCaml.
